# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Builders, makers, and product thinkers who pay attention to craft. They create software, write essays, ship products. They value signal over noise, slow observation over hot takes (even when they write hot takes). They find Product Hunt too gamified, dev Twitter too performative. They want a place that respects their intelligence.

## Product Purpose

Curious Geeks is a community platform for people who notice things. Members share products they've built and writing that captures their observations. No algorithms, no voting, no engagement metrics. Success looks like a handful of genuinely curious people discovering each other's work and finding it worth their attention.

## Brand Personality

Warm, opinionated, unhurried. Like a well-curated indie zine run by someone with good taste who also ships code. The brand voice is first-person direct, slightly dry, never corporate.

## Anti-references

- Product Hunt / Indie Hackers: vote counts, upvotes, startup-listing grids, orange/purple gradient SaaS clichés
- Generic blog templates: Bootstrap card grids, default Tailwind UI patterns, layout-first no-soul design
- Overdesigned editorial: precious whitespace, type-as-art, Medium-clones with no personality
- Dark mode tool aesthetic: terminal dark, dev-tool chrome, VS Code color scheme

## Design Principles

1. The community is the product — every surface should feel like it was made by and for real people, not a startup template
2. Restraint over decoration — remove before adding; earn complexity
3. Warmth through specificity — a hand-lettered feel, not a SaaS palette
4. Let the content breathe — the writing and products should be the visual protagonists, not the chrome
5. Confident choices, not safe ones — the brand has a point of view and commits to it

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline. No reduced motion exceptions needed beyond system preference respect. Color choices should maintain contrast against cream backgrounds.
